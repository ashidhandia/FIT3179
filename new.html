Cost of AI Evolution: Progress or Pitfall..?

With changing times, AI has swiftly integrated into our daily lives. Whether it’s the friendly voice on our phones or the buzz around generative AI, it is undeniable that artificial intelligence has come a long way. With exciting breakthroughs rolling out almost every month, AI is getting smarter and more capable. It's easy to get swept up in the waves of hype, but these shiny capabilities come at a real cost to society and our planet.


In the 60s, we witnessed a fierce nuclear arms race as superpowers poured resources into building up their arsenals. Fast forward to the 21st century, and we’re seeing a new kind of arms race—this time in artificial intelligence.

Countries are investing trillions to unlock AI’s potential for driving economic growth and technological progress. Take the United States, for instance. In 2021 alone, it funneled about $150 billion into AI development. To put that into perspective, that’s roughly the same as the entire GDP of Morocco!

As investments in AI continue to rise, we’ve seen a remarkable enhancement in its capabilities. Research by Kiela et al. (2023), along with insights from Our World in Data, sheds light on just how far AI has come over the years. In fact, the majority of these advancements have surpassed human benchmarks, showcasing the transformative potential of AI across various sectors.

According to the research by Patterson et al. (2021), the average energy consumption can be calculated through a set formula. It depends heavily on the number of parameters, as the more parameters there are, the longer and more intensive the training process becomes:
Energy (kWh) = Hours to train × Number of processors × Average power per processor (kW) × 1000 ÷ PUE.
The formula highlights how various factors such as training duration, the number of processors, and the power consumption of each processor contribute to the overall energy demand of training AI models.

This overuse of electricity is certainly harmful, which brings us to the concept of Earth Overshoot Day. Earth Overshoot Day marks the point in the year when humanity has consumed more resources than the Earth can regenerate in that same year. Since the late 1970s, this day has crept earlier and earlier on the calendar, signaling an unsustainable strain on the planet's resources. In 2023, it fell on August 2nd, demonstrating that we are consuming 75% more than what Earth's ecosystems can replenish.
