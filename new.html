Cost of AI Evolution: Progress or Pitfall..?

With changing times, AI has swiftly integrated into our daily lives. Whether it’s the friendly voice on our phones or the buzz around generative AI, it is undeniable that artificial intelligence has come a long way. With exciting breakthroughs rolling out almost every month, AI is getting smarter and more capable. It's easy to get swept up in the waves of hype, but these shiny capabilities come at a real cost to society and our planet.


In the 60s, we witnessed a fierce nuclear arms race as superpowers poured resources into building up their arsenals. Fast forward to the 21st century, and we’re seeing a new kind of arms race—this time in artificial intelligence.

Countries are investing trillions to unlock AI’s potential for driving economic growth and technological progress. Take the United States, for instance. In 2021 alone, it funneled about $150 billion into AI development. To put that into perspective, that’s roughly the same as the entire GDP of Morocco!

As investments in AI continue to rise, we’ve seen a remarkable enhancement in its capabilities. Research by Kiela et al. (2023), along with insights from Our World in Data, sheds light on just how far AI has come over the years. In fact, the majority of these advancements have surpassed human benchmarks, showcasing the transformative potential of AI across various sectors.

The impressive growth of AI capabilities is closely tied to increased investments. This surge in funding leads to improved GPUs, greater electricity consumption, and extended training periods, all of which contribute to the development of stronger and more capable AI models. But how exactly does this funding enhance performance? According to TEDai, the number of parameters in a model is a key factor in its effectiveness.

In recent years, we’ve seen a significant trend: AI models are growing by about an order of magnitude each year, meaning they’re becoming ten times larger annually. The upcoming OpenAI GPT-5 is expected to boast around 1 quadrillion parameters (that’s 100 followed by 15 zeros!). For context, the human brain has about 100 billion neurons, making GPT-5 10,000 times more complex in terms of parameters.

According to the research by Patterson et al. (2021), the average energy consumption for training AI models can be calculated using a set formula:
Energy (kWh) = Training Hours × Processors × Power/Processor × 1000 ÷ PUE
This consumption is directly tied to the model's complexity: the more parameters a model has, the longer and more intensive the training process becomes. As models grow larger and more powerful, understanding their energy footprint becomes crucial for balancing innovation with environmental responsibility.

This excessive power consumption raises concerns, leading us to the concept of Earth Overshoot Day. This day marks when humanity has used more resources than the Earth can regenerate within the year. Since the late 1970s, Earth Overshoot Day has been arriving earlier each year, highlighting the unsustainable pressure on our planet's resources.
In 2024, it fell on August 1st, indicating that we are currently using the resources of 1.75 Earths to sustain the consumption demands of humanity. We have exceeded the capacity for the Earth to regenerate itself well before the end of each year.

Alongside this excessive energy use and the planet's struggle to keep up, we’re also harming various ecosystems through our carbon emissions. Training AI models has an enormous carbon footprint, dwarfing the emissions of the average person.
